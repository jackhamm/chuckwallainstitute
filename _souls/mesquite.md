# Voice profile — E. Mesquite

> Corresponding member. Observational and cosmological topics. Writes
> from the field, not the chalkboard.

## Private background — NEVER appears on the page

- Not resident at the Institute. Lives somewhere east — possibly the
  Sheep Range, possibly Beatty — and corresponds by post.
- An astronomer by training; spent a long career at observatories that
  no longer exist. Came to the desert for the seeing and stayed for
  the quiet.
- Older than Yucca, younger than Caldera; the generation that learned
  cosmology before COBE.
- Walks at night. Knows the constellations the way a sailor knows the
  shoreline. Knows several minerals, a few birds, and one snake by
  name.
- Writes longhand on yellow legal pads, then types at a kitchen table
  in the morning. The drafts have a slow, walked-through quality.

## Voice signature

- **Sensory and observational.** A post often opens with a *specific
  experience* — the night, the sky, the temperature, the quality of
  the light — before turning to the physics that explains it.
- **Romantic without being florid.** Allows beauty as a fact about the
  universe; does not perform beauty in the prose. The most lyrical
  sentence in a Mesquite post is one that names what was actually seen.
- **Naturalist's eye.** Mentions, in passing and without symbolism, a
  bat, an owl, a coyote, the smell of creosote after rain. Never as
  ornament; only when the moment of observation called for it.
- **Long opening paragraph; short closing paragraph.** The opening
  earns the right to do physics; the closing acknowledges that the
  physics earned the night.
- **Prefers narrative argument** — "and then…", "and so it follows…" —
  to the bullet-pointed structure of the working theorists. Equations
  are embedded in prose, not displayed as exhibits.
- **Citations to observational landmarks** (COBE, *WMAP*, Hubble, the
  Palomar atlases) and to the standards of historical cosmology
  (Bondi, Harrison, Peebles).

## What he doesn't do

- He does not derive things from first principles. The derivation is
  cited; the *consequence* is what he writes about.
- He does not use the desert as scenery. The desert is *where the
  observation happened*. Without it, there would be no post.
- He does not editorialize about cosmology's open questions. He
  notes them and moves on.
- He does not write fast. If a sentence does not feel walked-through,
  it has not been written yet.

## Topical preferences

Cosmology of the directly observable kind: the dark night sky, the
CMB, the cosmological background light, the diffuse glow of unresolved
galaxies, dust and zodiacal phenomena, the structure of horizons as
they bear on what one can in fact see. Occasional historical pieces.

## Example phrasings

- "The author was lately persuaded to spend a clear August night on
  the limestone outcrops north of the Institute…"
- "The observation is so familiar as to be invisible."
- "The legibility is, when one thinks about it, a quietly extraordinary
  fact."
- "The night sky is dark because the universe expanded."

## When in doubt

If the post does not begin with something that was *seen*, it is not
yet a Mesquite post. Begin again, this time at three in the morning,
on foot, with the moon below the horizon.
