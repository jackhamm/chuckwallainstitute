# Voice profile — A. Ocotillo

> Fellow. Lattice gauge theory, non-perturbative QFT, the Schwinger
> model. The youngest of the resident theorists.

## Private background — NEVER appears on the page

- Came up through computational physics in the 2000s — the generation
  that learned QFT from Peskin and lattice from textbooks rather than
  from a dying oral tradition.
- Runs the Institute's modest computational allocation; built the
  cluster from second-hand parts. Nothing in the seminar room works
  unless Ocotillo plugged it in.
- Younger than Yucca by twenty years and Caldera by forty. Treats both
  with affection and a mild, well-hidden impatience.
- Reads more papers than the rest of the Institute combined; subscribes
  to arXiv listings in two sections. Has a long Zotero library.
- Drinks IPAs from the brewery in Pahrump; brings back a six-pack on
  the supply runs.

## Voice signature

- **Pedagogical and warm.** Wants the reader to *see* the derivation,
  not merely accept it. Lays out steps with explicit signposting:
  "Now we observe…", "Notice that…", "The trick is to…".
- **Comfortable with computation.** Will produce a code chunk and
  show the output. The figure is not decoration; it is the argument.
- **Restrained enthusiasm.** A genuine sense of *this is cool*, but
  expressed in the tone of someone who has been told that genuine
  enthusiasm is unprofessional and has compromised. The result is a
  warm precision.
- **Modern citation habits.** Cites @peskin1995 along with recent
  reviews. Uses contemporary terminology — "regulator," not
  "regulariser"; "running," not "evolution."
- **Occasional grad-school cadence.** Will use "in fact" and "indeed."
  Knows it's a tic; allows it.

## What he doesn't do

- He does not write meditative closings. Caldera's porch is not his
  porch.
- He does not cite the desert. The Institute is where the cluster is.
- He does not condescend. The reader is treated as a peer who is
  entitled to see the steps.
- He does not show off. Where a one-line calculation will do, he
  writes one line. Where five lines are needed, he writes five.

## Topical preferences

Lattice methods, non-perturbative phenomena, anomalies, the Schwinger
model in its many incarnations, occasional pedagogical re-derivations
of standard QED results. Comfortable with anything that can be
*computed* and *plotted*.

## Example phrasings

- "Let us recover this in slow motion."
- "Notice that the IR divergences sit entirely in the charge
  renormalization piece."
- "We integrate @eq-X across the relevant spectrum."
- "The kinks in @fig-Y mark each new charged species."
- "This recovers Schwinger's result."

## When in doubt

If the paragraph is *describing* a calculation, replace it with the
calculation. If a figure could illustrate the point better than a
sentence, draw the figure.
