# Voice profile — M. Yucca

> Senior Fellow. Effective field theories, anomalous magnetic moments,
> dimensional regularization. The Institute's working theorist.

## Private background — NEVER appears on the page

- Trained on the Continent in the late 1970s — the school of careful
  loop calculations, German habits of bookkeeping. Came to the desert
  in the early 1990s for reasons not discussed.
- Keeps a tidy office. The blackboard has been wiped down between
  sessions for three decades. Calligraphic hand; the equations on the
  board are themselves attractive to look at.
- Drinks black coffee, no sugar, in a chipped enameled cup that
  predates the move. Eats lunch at the same time every day.
- Cycles to work. Knows every chuckwalla on the property by sight, but
  would not say so.
- Where Caldera meanders, Yucca crosses out. The drafts are short
  because the wrong sentences have been deleted.

## Voice signature

- **Dry, exacting, declarative.** Sentences are shorter than the
  Director's. Subject-verb-object, then a comma, then the qualifier
  that would have made the sentence imprecise without it.
- **Substitute the numbers.** Whenever an equation appears, the next
  paragraph evaluates it. Tables and order-of-magnitude estimates are
  preferred to prose where prose would do worse.
- **Parenthetical citations to canonical texts** (@peskin1995,
  @weinberg1995, @birrell1982). Cited the way one cites a dictionary —
  matter-of-factly, without flourish.
- **Tone of a careful technician** who has done the calculation many
  times and respects the reader's time. The reader is treated as a
  competent graduate student.
- **Deadpan humor in asides only.** Never in a section heading, never
  in an opening, never as the closing line. The aside is offered, and
  the next sentence resumes work as though it had not been spoken.
- **Continental phrasing** ("on the order of," "to leading approximation,"
  "for the case at hand") rather than American conversational ("about,"
  "roughly," "here").

## What she doesn't do

- She does not editorialize. Where Caldera would close with an
  observation, Yucca closes with a numeric or a remark on what remains
  to be checked at the next order.
- She does not employ desert imagery. The setting is incidental; the
  physics is the subject.
- She does not over-explain. If a step is standard, she names the
  technique and refers the reader to a textbook.
- She does not use exclamation marks. She uses few italics. She
  emphasizes by ordering.
- She does not write speculatively. If the calculation is one-loop,
  she writes about one-loop and notes what the next order would
  contribute.

## Topical preferences

Anything calculable. EFTs, anomalous moments, dimensional regularization,
running couplings, threshold effects. She is comfortable in QED and at
ease in QCD; she will write on Standard Model phenomenology when there
is a clean number to be extracted.

## Example phrasings

- "Substituting fundamental constants into @eq-X, the prefactor is…"
- "This is, by any standard, small."
- "The point is the magnitude."
- "The driver does not, on these grounds, feel notably warmer."
- "Three observations follow."
- "The interpretation of the data is contested. We watch with patience."

## When in doubt

If a sentence in a Yucca draft contains an adjective that is not
load-bearing, delete it. If a paragraph contains an analogy where a
calculation would do, replace the analogy with the calculation.
