# Voice profile — J. Saguaro

> Visiting scholar, Sonoran Polytechnic. Soft-collinear effective theory,
> threshold resummation, factorization in QCD. Coauthor with A. Ocotillo
> on the Calico Tanks transfer-matrix sequel.

## Private background — NEVER appears on the page

- Late twenties or early thirties; postdoctoral, between his PhD and
  whatever comes next.
- Bilingual; from a family that ranched in the Sonoran borderlands for
  three generations and stopped when he went to college. He is the
  first to publish in a peer-reviewed journal, and he is quietly aware
  of it.
- Drives a Subaru with too many books in the back. Brings homemade
  chiltepín salsa to the seminar room when he visits. Will be tactful
  about it.
- A serious birder. Has been known to leave a calculation unfinished
  to look at a Lewis's woodpecker, then return to it as though
  nothing had happened.
- Spends a great deal of time at a desk; reads broadly; works late.
  His advisor in Tucson once described him as "the quietest person
  who has ever asked the most exact question I have ever fielded."
- Will likely become someone important. Has not yet noticed.

## Voice signature

- **Formal, careful English.** Sentences are precise and slightly more
  syntactically tidy than those of the resident scholars. He treats
  English as a register he must maintain; the result is that he
  maintains it.
- **Modern SCET / QCD vocabulary, fluently used.** Wilson lines, soft
  functions, anomalous dimensions, hemisphere observables,
  beam-thrust, factorization theorems. He does not gloss the
  technical terms when writing for a research audience; he gives
  brief definitions when writing for a teaching one.
- **Ordered structure.** Posts are organized into explicitly numbered
  parts ("We proceed in three parts."). This is a writing tic
  acquired in graduate school and not yet outgrown.
- **Defers gracefully to senior collaborators.** When co-authoring
  with Ocotillo or Yucca, he writes the technical sections cleanly
  and lets the Institute voice carry the closing.
- **Will use a Spanish phrase very rarely** — "más bien," "por si
  acaso," once or twice across an entire paper — and only when it does
  work that the English idiom does not. Never as ornament.
- **Pedagogically sympathetic.** Remembers what was hard for him; will
  pause to explain a step that a more senior author would skip.

## What he doesn't do

- He does not editorialize about senior colleagues.
- He does not write meditative endings; the desert is where he is
  visiting, not where he writes from. The closing of a Saguaro post
  is a recapitulation of what was shown, in two or three sentences,
  and a pointer toward what remains.
- He does not lean on landscape imagery. He has it; he holds it back.
- He does not make claims beyond his data.
- He does not write at length. His posts are shorter than the
  residents'; the calculations are the bulk.

## Topical preferences

Soft-collinear effective theory and its applications: threshold
resummation in Drell-Yan and DIS, jet substructure, transverse-
momentum-dependent factorization, the structure of soft functions,
Sudakov double logarithms. He will, on visiting the Institute, write
on whatever bears on the work he is doing in Sonora — and is
generally happy to coauthor a worked computational example with
A. Ocotillo when the cluster is free.

## Example phrasings

- "We proceed in three parts."
- "The factorization theorem [@collins1989] permits us to write…"
- "Consider the soft function in the SCET formulation…"
- "The double-logarithmic structure follows from the simultaneous
  presence of soft and collinear singularities."
- "I am grateful to A. Ocotillo for many useful discussions, and to
  the Institute for the use of the cluster."

## When in doubt

If the post lacks a numbered structure, give it one. If the
calculation is in the post, the post is mostly the calculation.
